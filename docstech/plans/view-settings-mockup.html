NoteThink · design exploration v3

The spine goes down, and the settings follow it

Grouped is a view, not a category heading. The left side shows the selected view's ancestor spine - the chain it inherits from - drawn top to bottom, because the settings descend that way too. The right side lists settings broadest-first: what comes from Grouped, then Line, then Kanban, each block aligned to its node on the spine. And because a real workspace has more views than fit, each variant has to keep saying other views exist. One of these is the selector-then-breadcrumb-then-options flow you described.

selected view abstract view (owns settings, cannot render) other views to switch to

Held constant: Kanban selected; its spine is Grouped → Line → Kanban; settings descend in that order. Only the way the spine is drawn and other views are surfaced changes.

Variant 1

Aligned spine

The spine is a vertical rail of the ancestor views on the left, each node sitting level with the settings it contributes on the right. A switcher at the top reaches every other view.

Variant 2

Selector, then breadcrumb, then options

Pick a view up top; that yields its spine as a short vertical breadcrumb; the options then render in exactly that order. The three stages are the mental model, laid out literally.

Variant 3

Descending settings, spine as a gutter

The settings lead, in descending order, each block headed by its view. A slim connected gutter on the left is the spine - the vertical breadcrumb reduced to a thread linking the levels.

Variant 4

Aligned spine, views on show

Variant 1's aligned spine, but instead of hiding the rest behind a click, a scrolling gallery keeps every other view visible - answering "there are more views" without breaking the alignment.

What changed from v2

  • Grouped is a view now, not a heading. The left side is the selected view's ancestor spine (Grouped → Line → Kanban), drawn vertically because the settings descend that way.
  • Right pane is broadest-first. Grouped's settings, then Line's, then Kanban's - each block tied to its spine node.
  • Alignment is tried literally in V1 and V4 (node level with its block); V3 reduces the spine to a thread; V2 is the selector → breadcrumb → options flow you asked for.
  • "Other views exist" is solved four ways: a switcher (V1, V3), a "3 more" pill (V2), and an always-visible gallery (V4).
  • Still assuming Kanban's Group by is fixed (edit by moving to Line). Open if you want it editable in place.
